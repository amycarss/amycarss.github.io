Next-Gen Engineers

There is a real race for talent, said John Maddox, president and CEO of the American Center for Mobility, or ACM, in Ypsilanti. Many companies have told us they’re looking to hire thousands of engineers over the next couple years globally.

In 2015, the state’s Connected and Autonomous Vehicle Task Force surveyed 50 employers in Southeast Michigan about the autonomous vehicle workforce. Their most common need was for “connected systems engineers” – a specialty that includes software engineering, systems engineering and electrical engineering, and pays roughly $90,000 a year.

Several entities in the region are working to establish a pipeline to these high-demand, high-paying jobs.

  • American Center for Mobility, co-founded by U-M,has brought together 15 universities and community colleges to work on the problem
  • U-M Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering offers a master’s degree in Transportation Systems Engineering and a PhD in Next Generation Transportation Systems.
  • The U-M Robotics Institute’s master’s and Ph.D. programs are ranked #2 in the nation, and the Institute will soon reside in the new Ford Motor Company Robotics Building
  • TechLab, which is run by the Center for Entrepreneurship, gives engineering undergraduates a chance to work with autonomous vehicle startups.
  • Center for Connected and Automated Transportation, a consortium of five Midwestern universities and Washtenaw Community College is developing courses and programs to educate tomorrow’s autonomous vehicle workforce
