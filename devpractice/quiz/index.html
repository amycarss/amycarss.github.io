Inclusive language: Faculty

Please be aware when you use the term faculty, it should be inclusive of all people classified as faculty. This includes:

  • Tenure-track faculty (tenured, tenure-track)
  • Research-track faculty (research professors, research scientists, research investigators)
  • Lecturers
  • Professors of practice (clinical professors)

Faculty is an inclusive term. Department websites present a very visible opportunity to reinforce inclusiveness, but this guideline should apply to all communications. So whenever you type the word “faculty,” stop for a moment and think about what you mean.

All four subcategories are included within “Faculty”.

Avoid using “faculty” when any of the four groups is excluded, and instead specify which groups you mean.

Core Faculty

Also, be careful about using the label “Core” as it relates to faculty. “Core” should not be used to distinguish one faculty group from another (for example, referring to only tenure-track faculty as Core Faculty and grouping them apart from Research Faculty and Lecturers). “Core” should relate to a faculty member’s level of appointment or activity within a department. (So, for example, a faculty member with a dry appointment might not be listed among Core Faculty, but a lecturer who teaches exclusively within your department would.)
